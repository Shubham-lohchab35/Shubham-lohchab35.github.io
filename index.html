About Me

I'm a self-driven student from India, applying to MIT in Early Action 2025. I love solving problems and building things—especially games and websites. I’m currently preparing to take the SAT in October 2025 and have been on a journey of deep self-growth, both academically and personally.

My Journey

During the pandemic, I spent a lot of time playing computer games. That’s where my curiosity about how games are made began—but it was just casual interest at first.

The turning point came in 10th grade, when I was diagnosed with severe liver cirrhosis. I was home-locked for many weeks, undergoing intense treatment and heavy medications. During those isolating days, computers became more than just entertainment—they became my escape, my teacher, and eventually my purpose. I started watching tutorials, learning to code, and building small things from scratch. That’s when I realized this wasn’t just a distraction—it was my passion.

Until Class 9, I wasn’t interested in mathematics either. But something changed that year—a great teacher, perhaps, or the joy of solving tough problems. I began enjoying math and even started helping my friends with their math and social studies homework. That experience gave me confidence and a sense of purpose in helping others too.

My journey has had both failures and wins: medical struggles, academic interruptions, emotional lows—but also personal projects, recovered motivation, tutoring friends, and self-learning. Each failure taught me something. Each achievement gave me hope.
